## Workout Management

Kinex Fit offers powerful and flexible workout management with multiple ways to create, import, edit, and track your training sessions.

---

### Adding a Workout

Click **"Add Workout"** from the dashboard or navigation to open the workout creation interface.

**[SCREENSHOT: Add workout page showing all tabs]**

You'll see **four tabs** at the top:

1. **Import from Instagram**
2. **Upload Image**
3. **Text Entry**
4. **Generate with AI**

---

#### Method 1: Import from Instagram

**[SCREENSHOT: Instagram import tab with URL field]**

Perfect for importing workouts from your favorite fitness influencers.

**Steps:**
1. Navigate to Instagram and find a workout post
2. Copy the post URL (e.g., `https://www.instagram.com/p/ABC123/`)
3. Paste the URL into the **"Instagram URL"** field
4. Click **"Import Workout"** button

**[SCREENSHOT: Loading state during import]**

**What Happens:**
- Kinex Fit fetches the post
- Extracts workout text from the caption
- Parses exercises, sets, and reps
- Creates a structured workout

**[SCREENSHOT: Successfully imported workout preview]**

5. Review the imported workout
6. Edit any details if needed
7. Click **"Save Workout"** to add it to your library

> **What You Can Do**: Instantly add professional workouts from Instagram without manual typing.
>
> **Pro Tip**: This works best with posts that have clear exercise lists in the caption.

---

#### Method 2: Upload Image (OCR)

**[SCREENSHOT: Image upload tab with upload zone]**

Upload screenshots or photos of workouts and let AI extract the text.

**Steps:**
1. Click the **"Upload Image"** tab
2. **Drag and drop** an image into the upload zone OR click **"Choose File"**

**[SCREENSHOT: File upload dialog]**

3. Select an image file (JPG, PNG, PDF supported)
4. Click **"Open"**

**[SCREENSHOT: Image processing with spinner]**

**What Happens:**
- Image is uploaded to AWS S3
- AWS Textract performs OCR (Optical Character Recognition)
- Extracted text is parsed into workout structure
- Preview generated

**[SCREENSHOT: OCR results showing extracted text and parsed workout]**

5. Review the extracted workout
6. Make any necessary corrections
7. Click **"Save Workout"**

> **What You Can Do**: Convert physical workout cards, screenshots, or photos into digital workouts.
>
> **Pro Tip**: For best results, use clear, high-contrast images with readable text.

---

#### Method 3: Text Entry

**[SCREENSHOT: Text entry tab with textarea and example format]**

Manually type or paste workout text for parsing.

**Steps:**
1. Click the **"Text Entry"** tab
2. Type your workout in the **large text area**

**Supported Formats:**
```
Exercise Name - Sets x Reps
Bench Press - 3 x 10
Squats - 4 x 8 @ 185lbs

OR

Exercise Name: Sets x Reps @ Weight
Deadlift: 5 x 5 @ 225 lbs
Overhead Press: 3 x 12

OR

Natural language:
3 sets of 10 push-ups
4 sets of squats with 8 reps at 185 pounds
```

**[SCREENSHOT: Filled text area with sample workout]**

3. Click **"Parse Workout"** button

**[SCREENSHOT: Parsed workout preview]**

**What Happens:**
- Text is analyzed
- Exercise names, sets, reps, and weights are extracted
- Structured workout is created

4. Review the parsed results
5. Edit if needed
6. Click **"Save Workout"**

> **What You Can Do**: Quickly enter workouts from any text source.
>
> **Pro Tip**: The parser is flexible and handles various formats. Don't worry about perfect formatting.

---

#### Method 4: Generate with AI

**[SCREENSHOT: AI generation tab with prompt field]**

Let Claude AI create a custom workout based on your description.

**Steps:**
1. Click the **"Generate with AI"** tab
2. Enter your workout request in natural language

**Example Prompts:**
- "Create a full body workout for strength"
- "Upper body push day with dumbbells only"
- "30-minute HIIT workout for fat loss"
- "Leg day with emphasis on quads, include squats and lunges"

**[SCREENSHOT: Prompt field with example text]**

3. Click **"Generate Workout"** button

**[SCREENSHOT: Generation in progress with loading indicator]**

**What Happens:**
- Your prompt is sent to Claude AI
- AI considers your training profile (experience, goals, equipment)
- Generates a complete workout with:
  - Exercise selection
  - Sets and reps
  - Weights/intensity recommendations
  - Form cues
  - Rest periods
  - Workout notes

**[SCREENSHOT: Generated workout preview showing full workout structure]**

4. Review the AI-generated workout
5. Click **"Enhance with AI"** if you want refinements (uses additional quota)
6. Click **"Save Workout"** to add to library

**AI Quota Indicator:**
**[SCREENSHOT: Quota display showing remaining requests]**

Your remaining AI requests are shown at the top (e.g., "3/10 requests remaining").

> **What You Can Do**: Create unlimited workout variations without planning knowledge.
>
> **Subscription Impact**:
> - **Free**: Limited AI requests per month
> - **Core/Pro**: More requests per month
> - **Elite**: Unlimited requests

> **Pro Tip**: Be specific in your prompts for best results. Mention your goals, equipment, time constraints, and any preferences.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Can I use Kinex Fit without internet?**
A: Partially. You can view cached workouts and complete sessions offline, but AI features and syncing require internet.

**Q: How is my data stored?**
A: Your data is stored in AWS DynamoDB with automatic backups. See Privacy Policy for details.

**Q: Can I export my data?**
A: Currently, you can view all your data in the app. Export functionality is planned for a future update.

**Q: Do you have an iOS/Android app?**
A: Not yet. The web app is mobile-optimized and can be added to your home screen as a Progressive Web App (PWA).

**Q: How do I add Kinex Fit to my phone's home screen?**

- **iOS**: Safari â†’ Share â†’ Add to Home Screen

A: All major credit cards via Stripe. More payment methods (PayPal, etc.) coming soon.

**Q: Can I cancel anytime?**
A: Yes! No commitments. Cancel anytime through Subscription settings. You'll keep access until the end of your billing period.

**Q: How does the AI know what workouts to create?**

**AMRAP**: As Many Reps/Rounds As Possible â€“ complete as much as you can in a time limit.

**1RM (One-Rep Max)**: The maximum weight you can lift for a single repetition. Estimated using the Epley formula.

**Body Metrics**: Measurements beyond weight, such as body fat percentage, waist circumference, etc.

**HIIT**: High-Intensity Interval Training â€“ alternating between intense bursts and rest periods.

**OCR**: Optical Character Recognition â€“ technology that extracts text from images.

**PR (Personal Record)**: Your best performance for a specific exercise (highest weight, most reps, etc.).

**Progressive Overload**: Gradually increasing weight, reps, or intensity over time to drive adaptation.

**Session**: A completed workout instance with logged sets, reps, and weights.

**Tabata**: A specific HIIT protocol: 20 seconds work, 10 seconds rest, 8 rounds.

**Volume**: Total weight lifted (Sets Ã— Reps Ã— Weight). Measures training load.

**Workout**: A planned set of exercises with prescribed sets and reps.
